Exploring K-Corporations at Seoul National University

January 2026

My exchange semester at Seoul National University was more than just a cultural experience; it was a deep dive into a completely different economic philosophy. Through the module "K-Corporations: A Social and Economic Perspective," I gained a new lens through which to view global business.

Coming from a background where Western business models—like those influenced by Taylor’s scientific management—often prioritize shareholder value, it was fascinating to see the South Korean "Chaebol" model in action.I learned how these massive corporations often place immense value on hierarchy and the decision-making power of founding families.

What struck me most was how these families maintain significant control despite often holding a small percentage of total shares. This contrast to the West, where shareholder rights are paramount, highlighted how deeply social values like loyalty and family legacy can influence economic structures.Understanding these nuances in corporate governance has given me a much broader perspective on how technology and business interact on a global scale.
